Getting Started

Are you feeling not good enough ?

You're in the right place. Slow down. Take a deep breath. Retreat into your own mind, body, and spirit. What you will find inside is a powerfully innate goodness.

When we suffer, fail or feel not good enough against impossible standards, there are practical tools to help us find our essential compassionate voice. The Mindful Self-Compassion course created by Drs. Kristin Neff and Chris Germer identifies a step by step approach to break free of negative self-talk and build inner strength by treating yourself in a kind, compassionate way.

Three key areas to build a strong foundation of self-compassion in your daily life:

    1. Mindfulness
    2. Common Humanity
    3. Self-Kindness

Mindfulness

Are you being present today?

Mindfulness is simply paying attention to the present moment as it unfolds with an attitude of non-judgment and curiosity. Sometimes we're so caught up in our endless to-do lists, our habitual state of achieving, planning, striving that we risk not being fully present in our lives. Instead, we can choose to show up for each moment in our life with openness.

Meditation can be considered the formal practice by which we train the mind to awaken to our own human experience.

When we are fully present, we can get in touch with our suffering. This takes courage, honesty and patience. At first, tapping into our suffering may sound unappealing but it's the only healthy way to heal. And it's well worth what you'll uncover.
