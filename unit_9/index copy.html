Getting Started

Do you often feel not good enough?

You weren't meant to doubt your abilities, act from a place of fear or treat yourself with an unforgiving harshness. You are worthy of much more.

When we suffer, fail or feel not good enough against impossible standards, there are practical tools to help us find our compassionate voice. The Mindful Self-Compassion course created by Drs. Kristin Neff and Chris Germer gives a step by step approach to break free of your negative self-talk and build inner strength by treating yourself in a kind, compassionate way.

Three areas to build a strong foundation of self-compassion into your daily life:

    1. Mindfulness
    2. Common Humanity
    3. Self-Kindness

Mindfulness

Are you fully present today?

Mindfulness is simply paying attention to the present moment as it unfolds with an attitude of non-judgment and curiosity. Sometimes we're so caught up in our endless to-do lists, our habitual state of achieving, planning, striving that we risk not being fully present in our lives. Instead, we can choose to show up for each moment in our life with openness.

Meditation can be considered the formal practice by which we train the mind to awaken to our own human experience.

When we are fully present, we can get in touch with our suffering. This takes courage, honesty and patience. At first, tapping into our suffering may sound unappealing but it's the only healthy way to heal. And it's well worth what you'll uncover.

Because when the noise settles down, you will soon discover a powerfully innate goodness to guide you.
